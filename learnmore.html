Basic template of an election and random voting model

An election happens at all the \(N\) electoral units following the first-post-the-past principle. Let the i-th electoral unit have \(n^e_i\) candidates and \(n^v_i\) eligible voters, where \(i = 1, 2, \cdots, N\).

Usually, only a fraction of the eligible voters cast their votes. This is termed the turnout \(T_i \le N\)

The candidate securing the highest number of votes, \(V_{w}\), is declared the winner, while the candidate with the second-highest votes, \(V_{r}\), is the runner-up.

The vote difference between the winner and the runner is the marin of victory \((M = V_w - V_r)\).

Specific margin, \(\mu = M / T \), is a turnout independent measure of electoral competition.

In the random voting model (RVM), electors cast their vote to j-th candidate, randomly with probablity \(p_j\). \(p_j\) is constructed by normalizeing random uniform weights \(w_j\).
